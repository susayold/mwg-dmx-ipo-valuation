# MWG After the DMX IPO

## IPO bridge, sum-of-the-parts valuation and the value of the non-DMX stub

**Independent analyst work sample**

**Data cut-off:** 13 July 2026

**Currency:** VND billion unless stated otherwise
**Status:** Valuation case study; no investment recommendation

---

## Executive summary

The completed primary offering of Dien May Xanh Investment JSC creates a visible value anchor for the consumer-electronics platform inside Mobile World Investment Corporation. The transaction does not, by itself, answer whether MWG is attractively valued. It changes the question.

The appropriate post-IPO framework is:

> MWG equity value = value of MWG's DMX stake + value of the non-DMX stub + parent-level adjustments.

The model deliberately separates four categories:

1. Official reported financial facts.
2. Like-for-like or pro-forma figures published by management.
3. Management guidance.
4. Independent analyst assumptions.

The supporting three-statement module standardizes 320 source-tagged facts across FY2023–FY2025 and Q1 2026, links the income statement, balance sheet and cash-flow statement through explicit bridges, and runs 35 accounting checks. FY2023 is correctly labelled as an unaudited comparative column in the FY2024 filing; FY2024 and FY2025 are audited; Q1 2026 is unaudited.

This separation matters because the DMX 2025 like-for-like perimeter excludes An Khang and AvaKids and includes DMX Technician. EraBlue remains inside DMX as an equity-accounted joint venture. Adding EraBlue again to the MWG stub would double-count value.

The completed offer issued 166.4385 million shares at VND 80,000 per share, raised VND 13,315.080 billion gross and increased DMX's outstanding shares to 1,267.722 million. The official close announcement describes MWG's post-offer holding as nearly 86%.

Operating evidence is constructive but not one-dimensional:

- Q1 2026 reported revenue increased 29.4% year on year to VND 32,542 billion.
- Reported gross margin rose to 19.18% from 17.99%.
- Reported NPAT increased 50.1% to VND 2,219 billion.
- CFO was only VND 864 billion, or 39% of NPAT, because working-capital movements absorbed cash.
- Six-month 2026 revenue reached VND 65,279 billion, equivalent to 53% of current annual guidance, with 32% same-store sales growth.

The evidence supports a deeper valuation exercise, but it does not support a Buy/Sell conclusion without a verified market-price date, exact diluted MWG share count at that date and a reviewed valuation of the non-DMX assets.

## 1. Transaction bridge

### 1.1 Planned versus actual close

| Item | Planned | Actual close |
|---|---:|---:|
| Offer price, VND/share | 80,000 | 80,000 |
| Pre-offer shares | 1,101,283,500 | 1,101,283,500 |
| Offered shares | 179,500,400 | 179,500,400 |
| Successfully issued shares | 179,500,400 assumed | 166,438,500 |
| Post-offer shares | 1,280,783,900 | 1,267,722,000 |
| Gross proceeds, VND bn | 14,360.032 | 13,315.080 |
| Estimated issue costs, VND bn | N/D | 100.000 |
| Estimated net proceeds, VND bn | N/D | 13,215.080 |
| Post-money value at offer price, VND bn | 102,462.712 | 101,417.760 |

The unsold 13,061,900 shares were cancelled. The actual bridge, not the planned offer size, must therefore drive post-money share count and valuation.

Resolution 15 discloses VND 13,215.080 billion of estimated net proceeds—VND 13,315.080 billion gross less VND 100.000 billion of estimated issuance costs—and a plan to use that amount to repay debt during 2026. The model treats this as a pro forma use-of-proceeds adjustment. It does not claim that the debt repayment had already been disbursed at the data cut-off.

### 1.2 Dilution logic

The model uses the following controls:

    Post-offer shares = pre-offer shares + successfully issued shares
    Gross proceeds = successfully issued shares × offer price
    Post-money equity = post-offer shares × offer price
    New-share dilution = successfully issued shares / post-offer shares

All four equations reconcile in the workbook. When the valuation starts from the 31 March 2026 pre-IPO balance sheet but uses post-IPO shares, estimated net proceeds of VND 13,215.080 billion are added once. If a later balance sheet already reflects the proceeds or repayment, the separate adjustment must be zero.

## 2. Corporate perimeter

### 2.1 Assets inside DMX

The official post-restructuring presentation places the following inside the DMX perimeter:

- The Gioi Di Dong.
- Dien May Xanh.
- TopZone.
- DMX Technician.
- Super App and related service initiatives.
- A 45% interest in PT Era Blu Elektronik, the EraBlue joint venture.

EraBlue is equity accounted. Its gross revenue is an operating KPI, while DMX's income statement records its share of the joint venture's profit. This has two valuation consequences:

- A P/E valuation based on DMX NPAT that already includes share of JV profit must not add EraBlue again.
- An FCFF DCF based on consolidated EBIT may need a separate fair value for the EraBlue stake at the equity bridge, because JV earnings do not enter consolidated EBIT in the same way.

### 2.2 Assets outside DMX

The non-DMX MWG stub includes:

- Bach Hoa Xanh through its separate ownership branch.
- An Khang through the Thien Tam ownership branch.
- AvaKids through the An Nhi ownership branch.
- Parent cash, debt, investments, costs and other corporate adjustments.

The project does not present an implied BHX valuation as a verified market conclusion until the observed MWG market capitalisation and date are supplied.

## 3. Latest financial evidence

### 3.1 Q1 2026 income statement

| Item | Q1 2025 | Q1 2026 | Change |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Net revenue | 25,154 | 32,542 | +29.4% |
| Gross profit | 4,526 | 6,241 | +37.9% |
| Gross margin | 17.99% | 19.18% | +119 bps |
| Profit before tax | 1,857 | 2,775 | +49.5% |
| NPAT | 1,478 | 2,219 | +50.1% |
| Net margin | 5.88% | 6.82% | +94 bps |

Revenue growth without a comparable expansion of the mature Vietnam store network suggests that same-store productivity and category mix are important drivers. Margin growth indicates that the quality-growth strategy produced operating leverage during the quarter.

### 3.2 Cash conversion

Q1 2026 CFO was VND 864 billion versus VND 2,219 billion of NPAT. The 39% conversion ratio is not, by itself, evidence of weak earnings quality because retail working capital is seasonal. It is a control point.

The cash-flow statement shows:

- Inventory increased.
- Payables decreased.
- Tax and interest payments absorbed cash.

The model therefore stresses inventory days, supplier terms and financed-sales growth rather than forecasting CFO as a fixed percentage of NPAT.

### 3.3 Balance-sheet liquidity

At 31 March 2026:

- Cash and cash equivalents were approximately VND 3,313 billion.
- Short- and long-term held-to-maturity deposits were approximately VND 26,755 billion.
- Short-term borrowings were approximately VND 22,159 billion.
- Net cash-like liquidity before other adjustments was approximately VND 7,909 billion.

This is a simplified liquidity bridge, not a final excess-cash adjustment. Restricted balances, operating cash needs, lease treatment and IPO proceeds must be considered before the balance is used in valuation.

### 3.4 Six-month operating update

The unaudited six-month 2026 newsletter reported:

- Revenue of VND 65,279 billion.
- Revenue growth of 31% year on year on the stated like-for-like basis.
- Same-store sales growth of 32%.
- 53% completion of annual revenue guidance of VND 122,500 billion.
- 261 EraBlue stores and 92% year-on-year EraBlue revenue growth.
- Financed sales at 38% of DMX revenue.

These KPIs are useful forecast inputs but do not replace Q2/H1 financial statements, which were not yet available at the project cut-off.

## 4. Three-statement analysis

### 4.1 Historical coverage and assurance

| Period | Income statement | Balance sheet | Cash flow | Assurance status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| FY2023 | Full year | 31 December 2023 | Full year | Comparative in FY2024 filing; expressly unaudited |
| FY2024 | Full year | 31 December 2024 | Full year | Audited consolidated |
| FY2025 | Full year | 31 December 2025 | Full year | Audited consolidated |
| Q1 2026 | Three months YTD | 31 March 2026 | Three months YTD | Unaudited consolidated |
| H1 2026 | Operating KPIs only | — | — | No full statement set at cut-off |

Q1 2025 comparative flow figures are used for year-on-year analysis where disclosed. Q1 is a latest-period trend update, not a substitute for full-year evidence on seasonality, inventory cycles, margin durability, CapEx/depreciation or debt policy.

### 4.2 Three-year trend

| VND bn, except margins | FY2023 | FY2024 | FY2025 | Q1 2026 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Revenue | 86,822.1 | 93,356.6 | 109,479.2 | 32,542.0 |
| Gross profit | 14,418.5 | 17,089.4 | 19,486.7 | 6,241.2 |
| Gross margin | 16.61% | 18.31% | 17.80% | 19.18% |
| Operating profit | 2,004.0 | 5,084.0 | 7,240.4 | 2,772.7 |
| Operating margin | 2.31% | 5.45% | 6.61% | 8.52% |
| NPAT attributable to parent | 1,345.5 | 3,716.6 | 5,801.8 | 2,218.6 |
| CFO | 2,357.3 | 6,243.1 | 5,016.7 | 863.7 |
| CFO / NPAT | 175.2% | 168.0% | 86.5% | 38.9% |

The margin trend indicates genuine operating improvement rather than a profit increase explained solely by financial income. The cash-flow trend is less linear: cash conversion exceeded earnings in FY2023–FY2024, fell below earnings in FY2025 and weakened sharply in Q1 2026.

### 4.3 Linked-statement bridges

The model reconciles three accounting relationships:

    NPAT + non-cash items ± working-capital movements − interest/tax paid = CFO

    Opening cash + CFO + CFI + CFF + FX effects = Closing cash

    Opening retained earnings + NPAT − dividends
    ± capitalisation/other equity movements = Closing retained earnings

All 35 generated accounting checks pass. In FY2025, the retained-earnings bridge contains VND 6,542.278 billion of capitalisation and other equity movements related to the disclosed restructuring. The analysis isolates that residual rather than treating it as operating income, cash flow or an invented normalization adjustment.

The Q1 2026 NPAT-to-CFO bridge is especially informative. Reported NPAT was VND 2,218.6 billion, while CFO was VND 863.7 billion. The bridge includes:

- VND 434.8 billion of cash absorbed by inventory;
- VND 466.0 billion absorbed by lower payables;
- VND 79.6 billion absorbed by receivables;
- VND 780.3 billion of corporate income tax paid; and
- non-cash and investing-profit adjustments that reconcile the remainder.

### 4.4 Working-capital schedule

    CCC = DIO + DSO − DPO

| Metric | FY2024 | FY2025 | Q1 2026 YTD |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| DIO | 88.0 days | 83.6 days | 78.4 days |
| DSO | 1.1 days | 1.1 days | 1.0 days |
| DPO | 28.3 days | 32.5 days | 31.3 days |
| Cash conversion cycle | 60.8 days | 52.1 days | 48.0 days |
| Inventory provision / gross inventory | 2.24% | 2.61% | 3.15% |
| CFO / NPAT | 168.0% | 86.5% | 38.9% |
| CapEx / revenue | 0.08% | 0.12% | 0.36% |

The schedule uses average opening and closing trade balances and actual elapsed days: 366 for FY2024, 365 for FY2025 and 90 for Q1 2026. The apparent Q1 CCC improvement is therefore a seasonal observation, not a full-year run rate. DSO near one day is consistent with a cash-heavy retail model; it does not prove that DMX carries no economic exposure to financed sales without reviewing lender and recourse terms.

The supplier-funding swing is more important than the headline CCC alone. Payables contributed VND 2,104.9 billion to Q1 2025 CFO but absorbed VND 466.0 billion in Q1 2026. A sustained reduction in supplier funding would pressure liquidity even if revenue and NPAT continue to grow.

### 4.5 Quality of earnings

| Indicator | Observation | Interpretation | Risk / next evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue growth | Q1 2026 revenue +29.4% YoY | Strong operating momentum | One quarter does not establish sustainability |
| Gross margin | Expanded by about 119 bps YoY | Mix, pricing and operating leverage appear supportive | Promotions, category mix and vendor support may normalize |
| CFO / NPAT | 38.9%, down from 167.6% in Q1 2025 | Working capital and tax explain the main gap | H1/full-year statements are needed before judging earnings quality |
| Payables | VND 466.0bn cash outflow | Supplier financing reversed | Lower funding could create cash pressure |
| Inventory provision | Coverage rose to 3.15% | More inventory risk is recognized | Ageing and category obsolescence remain undisclosed |
| Net finance contribution / PBT | Approximately 6.7% | Treasury income supports but does not dominate profit | Deposit income may change after debt repayment |

The correct conclusion is not that Q1 earnings are low quality. It is that profit growth has not yet converted into cash on the same basis, and the seasonal working-capital bridge must be revisited when H1 and FY2026 statements become available.

### 4.6 Normalization policy

| FY2025 reported item | Adjustment | Normalized/comparator item | Reason |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Revenue 109,479.188 | (2,479.188) | Management LFL revenue 107,000.000 | Disclosed post-restructuring comparator; statutory actual remains unchanged |
| NPAT parent 5,801.785 | +273.215 | Management LFL NPAT 6,075.000 | Only the disclosed headline is bridged |
| Operating profit 7,240.396 | N/D | N/D | Disclosure is not granular enough to normalize EBIT |
| Cash 3,578.155 | N/D | N/D | Minimum operating cash is not disclosed |
| Debt 23,429.114 | N/D | N/D | Lease and adjusted-net-debt basis is not sufficiently disclosed |

Knowing when **not** to adjust is an important control. The model does not invent one-offs, excess cash, lease treatment or line-by-line carve-out allocations merely to make the valuation look cleaner.

### 4.7 Senior analyst read-through

1. Earnings improvement is supported by operating-margin expansion, not only by financial income.
2. FY2025 inventory growth was substantially funded by payables, while Q1 2026 supplier funding reversed.
3. Low trade receivables do not answer who ultimately bears financed-sales credit risk.
4. Rising inventory-provision coverage is directionally prudent, but ageing data are still needed.
5. Cash plus deposits less debt is a liquidity indicator, not automatically distributable excess cash.
6. IPO proceeds are primary capital of DMX, not cash received by MWG; the debt-repayment allocation remains pro forma until execution is evidenced.

## 6. Valuation architecture

### 6.1 DMX

The model supports two primary methods:

1. FCFF DCF with an explicit bridge for non-operating assets and EraBlue.
2. Earnings-multiple valuation based on normalised NPAT-MI.

The website's interactive lab uses the earnings method for transparency:

    DMX equity value = forecast NPAT × selected P/E
    MWG share of DMX = DMX equity value × MWG ownership

The default scenario is an illustration, not a recommendation.

### 6.2 MWG SOTP

    MWG equity value
      = MWG share of DMX
      + BHX value
      + An Khang value
      + AvaKids value
      + other assets
      + parent net cash/debt and corporate adjustments

The workbook keeps gross asset value and parent adjustments in separate rows. It never hides a holding-company discount inside a business-unit multiple.

### 6.3 Publication gate

The following outputs remain blank or explicitly illustrative until all required inputs are supplied:

- Observed MWG market capitalisation.
- Implied value of the non-DMX stub.
- Upside or downside.
- Target price.
- Investment rating.

Required inputs are a verified share price, price date, diluted MWG share count at that date and a source.

## 7. Scenario design

### Bear

- Lower mature-store sales growth.
- Gross margin fails to sustain Q1 improvement.
- Higher inventory days and weaker supplier funding.
- Lower EraBlue contribution.
- Lower valuation multiple.
- Larger parent-level deduction.

### Base

- Revenue progresses broadly in line with current management guidance.
- Gross margin expands gradually.
- Working-capital conversion normalises after seasonal Q1 pressure.
- EraBlue scales while remaining equity accounted.
- The non-DMX stub is valued separately using transparent assumptions.

### Bull

- Strong same-store growth persists.
- Service and financial-touchpoint mix expands margin.
- EraBlue delivers faster store productivity and profit contribution.
- Working capital remains controlled.
- Higher earnings and a higher valuation multiple reinforce each other.

| Scenario | DMX NPAT | P/E | DMX equity | MWG share at ~86% | Non-DMX stub | Parent adjustments | Illustrative MWG equity |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Bear | 6,700 | 10.0x | 67,000 | 57,620 | 28,000 | (8,000) | 77,620 |
| Base | 7,350 | 12.3x | 90,405 | 77,748.3 | 45,000 | (5,000) | 117,748.3 |

The scenario table is intentionally two-dimensional where possible. Combining simultaneous optimistic assumptions without sensitivity analysis would overstate confidence.

These are VND billion equity illustrations, not target prices. The model does not convert them into value per share, premium/upside or a rating while exact ownership, same-date diluted shares and market price remain unverified on one basis.

## 8. Catalysts and monitoring points

### Potential catalysts

1. First trading and subsequent price discovery for DMX.
2. Q2/H1 2026 financial statements.
3. Evidence that the planned debt repayment with IPO proceeds was executed and produces durable interest savings and improved capital efficiency.
4. Further disclosure on EraBlue unit economics.
5. Sustained BHX profitability and cash generation.

### Key risks

1. Consumer-electronics demand normalises after a strong comparative period.
2. Gross-margin expansion proves temporary.
3. Financed-sales growth raises credit-partner or regulatory dependency.
4. Working capital absorbs more cash than expected.
5. EraBlue expansion prioritises store count over unit economics.
6. Holding-company complexity creates persistent valuation discount.
7. Model users double-count EraBlue, IPO cash or group net debt.
8. Reported, LFL and post-restructuring perimeters are mixed.

## 9. Conclusion

The DMX IPO makes MWG more analyzable, not automatically more valuable. The transaction provides a concrete post-money reference and a clean starting point for a sum-of-the-parts framework.

The strongest current evidence is the combination of fast revenue growth and margin expansion. The most important counterweight is cash conversion, particularly the Q1 reversal in supplier funding. The most important modelling controls are the corporate perimeter and a consistent pre-/post-IPO balance-sheet basis.

The project therefore reaches a methodological conclusion:

> Value DMX once, keep EraBlue inside that perimeter, value the non-DMX assets separately, and publish a market conclusion only after the price-date and diluted-share controls are satisfied.

It intentionally does not issue an investment rating.

The current work proves three-statement understanding through historical reconciliation, working-capital schedules and earnings-quality analysis. It intentionally stops short of claiming a fully integrated three-statement forecast.
